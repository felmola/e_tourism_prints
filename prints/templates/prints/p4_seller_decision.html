Additionally, you can choose a commercial practice to help make your package more attractive in the market.

There are three commercial practices available that buyers will be able to use to make their package more attractive to the market.

Commercial Practice Definition Example
Best Price Guarantee This commercial practice allows you to make the announcement that, among all packages with the same destination, this one has the lowest price. Suppose you offer the package to Palau. There may be other sellers in the market selling the same package to Palau, but if you opt for this commercial practice, you would be saying that, of all the packages to Palau, yours is the cheapest.
Reference Pricing This commercial practice allows you to make the announcement that between the first price you proposed and the end, you offered a discount. Suppose you offer a package to Palau. You can claim that you made a discount between the first price you bid of 6 EMU and the final (the one you confirmed after deciding whether or not to observe the competition)
Drip pricing This commercial practice allows you to display a price for the package you are bidding that is less than the total price you are asking for it. The difference between the actual price and the price you are displaying is equal to the cost for viewing the full list of packages (0.2 EMU), regardless of whether you decided to pay this cost or not. Suppose you offer a package to Palau and the price you claim for this package is 8 UME. If you decide to use this commercial practice, your offer will appear with a price of 7.8* UME, with a note indicating the total price.

Notice that these commercial practices can be used truthfully (case 1) or untruthfully (case 2). Using the examples in the table above, in the following table you will find each of the commercial practices and the examples for case 1 and case 2.

Commercial Practice Case 1 (truthful) Case 2 (untruthful)
Best Price Guarantee 1) When there is no other package to Palau. 2) When there exists at least another package to Palau and the latter has a larger price. 1) When there exists at least another package to Palau and the latter has a smaller price.
Reference Pricing 1) Use of the real discount. 1) Use of the real discount plus 2 UME
Drip pricing 1) When the seller shows the Price minus the cost to see the first offer of the other sellers. 1) When the seller decides not to see the prices of the competitors but still show the price net of this cost

Remember that it is your decision whether or not to use any of these business practices.
