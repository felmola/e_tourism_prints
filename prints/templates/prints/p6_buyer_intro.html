You were assigned to the role of buyer. Remember that you will keep this role during the 5 trading rounds. You will be able to buy only one package per trading round. The computer will give you a "value" for each of the packages you will find for sale in the market. This value represents how much you value that package if you were to close a transaction. In total there are 5 types of tourist packages whose only difference will be the destination: Berlin, Madrid, Vienna, Paris and Rome. You will only receive five values, one for each package.

Please note that the values are private, i.e. only you know this numbers. Neither the other buyers nor the sellers know this information.

You will be shown a list of all package offers in the market and their respective prices. To close a transaction, you must select one and only one of the packages in the market. Your earnings will be calculated as the difference between your valuation and the price at which you purchased the package. If you fail to complete a transaction, you won’t make any profit.

Think of it this way, suppose you have the ability to resell each of the five packages in question, and if you manage to get one, you will earn the difference. The value is what you would receive when you resell the package. If the value is 7 ECU and you manage to get the package at a price of 5 ECU, then your profit would be 2 ECU. If you do not succeed in buying the package, your profit would be zero, i.e. you would not pay any price and would not receive any income.

Let's see another example:

Suppose that the value for the package you decide to buy is 5 ECU and you manage to buy the package at a price of 3 ECU. You will then earn 5 ECU - 3 ECU = 2 ECU. If you fail to close a transaction, you will incur no cost or profit, i.e. your profit will be 0 ECU.

It is important to note that two buyers can select the same offer but only one of them will complete the transaction. The one who makes the decision to buy the package the fastest will be the one who completes the transaction.

----> Conditionally on: Commercial Practice Treatment <----

Additionally, notice that the sellers can choose a commercial practice to help make their packages more attractive in the market. There are three commercial practices and if a seller uses them, they will be visible to the buyer at the time of completing the transaction. However, keep in mind that any of these commercial practices can be used truthfully or untruthfully.

----> Conditionally on: Informal Sanction Treatment <----

In the end, the buyers will be able to report a seller if they think he/she used the practice untruthfully. If at least 3 buyers report that a specific seller used any business practice untruthfully, that seller will have to pay a fine. This fine will be 0.6 ECU plus 0.2 ECU for each additional buyer who reports it. The amount of the fine will be divided equally among the buyers who reported the seller.

----> Conditional Start: Formal Sanction Treatment <----

In the end, we will audit two of the sellers randomly, i.e. the probability of being audited is 20%. If an audited seller is using some commercial practice untruthfully, the seller must pay 2 ECU as a fine. These 2 ECU will be distributed among the buyers.

----> Conditional Start: Regret Treatment <----

Please note that buyers will be given information about the price of the package they want to purchase before completing the transaction. If the price of the package is above the average, a message will appear with a sad emoticon indicating that. If, on the other hand, the price of the package is lower than or equal to the average, a happy emoticon will appear indicating that.

----> Conditional Start: All treatment but control <----

There are three commercial practices available that buyers will be able to use to make their package more attractive to the market.

Commercial Practice Definition Example
Best Price Guarantee This commercial practice allows the seller to make the announcement that, among all packages with the same destination, this one has the lowest price. Suppose the seller offers the package to Rome. There may be other sellers in the market selling the same package to Rome, but if the seller opts for this commercial practice, the seller would be saying that, of all the packages to Rome, hers/his is the cheapest.
Reference Pricing This commercial practice allows the seller to make the announcement that between the first price the seller proposed and the end, the seller offered a discount. Suppose the seller offers a package to Rome. The seller can claim that she/he made a discount between the first price she/he bid of 6 ECU and the final (the one the seller confirmed after deciding whether or not to observe the competition)
Drip pricing This commercial practice allows the seller to display a price for the package she/he is bidding that is less than the total price she/he is asking for it. The difference between the actual price and the price the seller is displaying is equal to the cost for viewing the full list of packages (0.2 ECU), regardless of whether the seller decided to pay this cost or not. Suppose the seller offers a package to Rome and the price the seller claims for this package is 8 ECU. If the seller decides to use this commercial practice, the seller's offer will appear with a price of 7.8 ECU*, with a note indicating the total price.

Notice that these commercial practices can be used truthfully (case 1) or untruthfully (case 2). Using the examples in the table above, in the following table you will find each of the commercial practices and the examples for case 1 and case 2.

Commercial Practice Case 1 (truthful) Case 2 (untruthful)
Best Price Guarantee 1) When there is no other package to Rome. 2) When there exists at least another package to Rome and the latter has a larger price. 1) When there exists at least another package to Rome and the latter has a smaller price.
Reference Pricing 1) Use of the real discount. 1) Use of the real discount plus 2 ECU
Drip pricing 1) When the seller shows the price minus the cost to see the first offer of the other sellers. 1) When the seller decides not to see the prices of the competitors but still show the price net of this cost

Remember that it is the seller’s decision whether or not to use any of these commercial practices.
